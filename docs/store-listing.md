## Detailed description (main listing body)

```
Voice Bubble adds a small, draggable microphone bubble to every page. Click any
text box, press the mic, and just talk — your words are typed straight into that
field, live. Built for hands-free and one-handed use, but handy any time talking
is faster than typing.

WHAT IT DOES
• Types into whatever field you click — search boxes, forms, textareas, and rich
  editors (works with modern web apps like Gmail, Docs-style editors, chat apps).
• A floating bubble you can drag anywhere; it remembers where you put it.
• 18 languages, switchable on the fly.
• Auto-stops after a configurable pause, so you don't have to reach for "stop".
• Append to a field or replace it on each dictation.
• Per-field buttons: record, clear the field, and release it.

HOW IT WORKS
Voice Bubble uses your browser's built-in speech recognition. The microphone is
requested once and then works on every site — no re-prompting as you move around
the web.

PRIVACY
• No accounts, no tracking, no servers of our own.
• Your settings (language, pause time, mode) and the bubble's position are stored
  locally in your browser.
• Speech is transcribed by the browser's built-in Web Speech API, which sends
  audio to the browser vendor's speech service for recognition — the same service
  any website's voice input uses. Voice Bubble adds nothing on top of that.

WHY IT EXISTS
I built Voice Bubble for my girlfriend after she dislocated a finger and spent
weeks with her arm in a cast, which made typing slow and painful. I hope it makes
working at a laptop a little easier for you too.

TIP
Click a text box, press the mic in the bubble (or the small record button that
appears inside the field), and start talking.
```

## Permission justifications (Privacy practices tab)

- **`storage`** — Stores the user's settings (language, auto-stop time, insert
  mode, on/off) and the bubble's position locally so they persist between
  sessions.
- **Host access on all sites (`<all_urls>`)** — The mic bubble must be available
  in text fields on any website the user chooses to dictate into; the extension
  runs a small in-page UI and inserts the recognized text into the focused field.
- **Web-accessible recognizer page** — Speech recognition runs in a hidden page
  served from the extension's own origin so microphone permission is granted once
  and reused on every site, instead of re-prompting per website.
- **Microphone (requested at runtime)** — Captures the user's speech to transcribe
  it into the selected text field. Audio is used only for live transcription and
  is not stored or sent to the developer.

---

## Data disclosures (data-usage checkboxes)

- The extension does **not** collect or transmit data to the developer (no
  analytics, no backend).
- Audio **does** leave the device — but to the **browser's** speech service
  (Web Speech API), not ours. Declare that the extension handles "audio"
  transiently for its core function and does not sell or transfer it. Do **not**
  claim "no data leaves the device," since the Web Speech API sends audio to
  Google for recognition.
